This study note helps you prepare IELTS Speaking Part 1 answers for the topic of bicycles. Keep your answers personal, direct, and natural.

# Part 1 Bicycles

### Ideas Bank

- whether you own a bike
- when and where you cycle
- safety and cycling infrastructure
- cycling culture in your country
- why cycling should or should not be encouraged

### Part 1 Prompt Starters

- Do you have a bike?
- How often do you use it?
- How old were you when you learned to ride a bike?
- Do you think using bicycles should be encouraged?

### Sample Short Questions & Model Answers

#### Q1: Do you have a bike?

Model answer: In short, yes. In day-to-day life, using cycle lanes for short trips in my area.

#### Q2: How often do you use it?

Model answer: Fairly often, especially on normal weekdays. A good example is using cycle lanes for short trips in my area.

#### Q3: How old were you when you learned to ride a bike?

Model answer: To me, it is clearly part of daily life. A simple example is using cycle lanes for short trips in my area.

### Study Tips

- Give one short personal example in each answer.
- Use 1 to 2 paraphrases naturally instead of repeating the same words.
- Keep each answer around two to four sentences so it sounds natural in the test.
