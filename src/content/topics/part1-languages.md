This study note helps you prepare IELTS Speaking Part 1 answers for the topic of languages. Keep your answers personal, direct, and natural.

# Part 1 Languages

### Ideas Bank

- languages you can speak or are learning
- your first foreign-language learning experience
- why language learning matters
- common difficulties for learners
- how to practice effectively

### Part 1 Prompt Starters

- How many foreign languages do you speak?
- When did you start learning your first foreign language?
- How many languages do children in your country learn at school?
- Do you think it is important to learn a foreign language?

### Sample Short Questions & Model Answers

#### Q1: How many foreign languages do you speak?

Model answer: I would say it matters more than people think. In practical terms, practicing speaking with short daily conversations.

#### Q2: When did you start learning your first foreign language?

Model answer: I generally choose times and places that are practical. A typical example is practicing speaking with short daily conversations.

#### Q3: How many languages do children in your country learn at school?

Model answer: From my perspective, it is quite relevant in everyday life. For instance, practicing speaking with short daily conversations.

### Study Tips

- Give one short personal example in each answer.
- Use 1 to 2 paraphrases naturally instead of repeating the same words.
- Keep each answer around two to four sentences so it sounds natural in the test.
