This study note helps you prepare IELTS Speaking Part 1 answers for the topic of dreams. Keep your answers personal, direct, and natural.

# Part 1 Dreams

### Ideas Bank

- how often you remember dreams
- difference between dreams and daydreams
- whether dreams reflect stress
- dreams you can still recall clearly
- if dreams influence your mood

### Part 1 Prompt Starters

- Do you often have dreams when you sleep?
- Do you usually remember your dreams?
- Do you ever have daydreams?
- What kind of daydreams do you usually have?

### Sample Short Questions & Model Answers

#### Q1: Do you often have dreams when you sleep?

Model answer: In short, yes. In day-to-day life, remembering vivid dreams when I am stressed or tired.

#### Q2: Do you usually remember your dreams?

Model answer: Overall, yes, in most situations. A good example is remembering vivid dreams when I am stressed or tired.

#### Q3: Do you ever have daydreams?

Model answer: Yes, generally I would say so. For example, remembering vivid dreams when I am stressed or tired.

### Study Tips

- Give one short personal example in each answer.
- Use 1 to 2 paraphrases naturally instead of repeating the same words.
- Keep each answer around two to four sentences so it sounds natural in the test.
