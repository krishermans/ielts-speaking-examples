Urban planning is a useful IELTS topic because it connects to housing, transport, green spaces, public services, and the overall quality of life in a city. It may also appear under the name urban design. In speaking, it helps to give balanced opinions about how cities should grow and what makes them practical, attractive, and liveable.

# Urban Planning

### The Main Idea

Urban planning is the process of deciding how space in a city should be used. It includes where homes, offices, schools, parks, and roads should be located, as well as how to manage future growth. Good planning can reduce congestion, protect historic areas, and make daily life more convenient for residents.

### Sample Short Questions & Model Answers

#### Q1: Why is urban planning important?

Model answer: It is absolutely essential, because unplanned growth usually creates long-term problems. Effective planning coordinates housing, transport, and public services, which makes cities more efficient and far less stressful to live in.

#### Q2: Would you like to live in a crowded city?

Model answer: Not in the long run, because constant noise and congestion can be mentally draining. Having said that, big cities often provide stronger career prospects, better public transport, and more cultural options, so the trade-off can be worth it for some people.

#### Q3: Should cities focus more on beauty or functionality?

Model answer: I would argue for a balance, but with functionality as the foundation. If transport and services do not work, visual appeal alone is meaningless; however, thoughtful design and attractive public spaces can still improve well-being and civic pride.

#### Q4: Is it a good idea to replace old buildings with modern ones?

Model answer: It depends entirely on context. Buildings with historical or cultural significance should be preserved where possible, but replacing unsafe or highly inefficient structures can be justified if the new development serves community needs.

#### Q5: What makes a city pleasant to live in?

Model answer: For me, liveability comes down to accessibility, safety, and balance. A pleasant city offers reliable transport, walkable neighbourhoods, and sufficient green space, while also keeping housing reasonably affordable for ordinary residents.

### Essay Or Discussion Questions

#### Topic 1: Beauty vs Function

Question: Some people think cities should be designed to be beautiful, while others believe they should be designed to be functional. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

Points to cover:
- explain why practical design matters for transport, housing, and public services
- consider how attractive architecture and parks can improve quality of life
- give a balanced opinion about combining both aims

#### Topic 2: Green Space vs Housing

Question: Some people think urban areas need more green spaces, while others believe more housing is the priority. What is your opinion?

Points to cover:
- discuss pressure on housing in growing cities
- explain the health and environmental benefits of parks and trees
- suggest how cities could create a better balance

#### Topic 3: Old Buildings vs New Development

Question: Some people believe old buildings should be replaced by modern ones, while others think older buildings still have value. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

Points to cover:
- explain why modern buildings can be safer and more efficient
- consider the cultural and historical importance of older structures
- state when renovation is better than demolition

### Study Tips

- Use clear topic words such as infrastructure, green spaces, heritage, and congestion.
- When you give an opinion, try to balance the needs of residents, business, and the environment.
- In speaking, simple examples about your own city can make your answers sound more natural and convincing.
